Senna auriculata (Avaram Poo)

Common Names: Avaram Poo, Tanner's Cassia, Matura Tea Tree

Scientific Classification

Scientific Name: Senna auriculata (L.) Roxb.
Family: Fabaceae (Caesalpinioideae)
Type: Evergreen shrub / small tree
Native Range: Indian subcontinent & Sri Lanka

Description

A vibrant, drought-tolerant shrub with glossy pinnate leaves and abundant, bright yellow five-petaled flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it popular in gardens, along roadsides, and as a hedge plant.

The flowers bloom in clusters and attract numerous pollinators, including bees and butterflies. The plant also produces flattened brown pods containing seeds.

Ornamental Uses

  • Beautiful yellow flowers for gardens and parks
  • Hedging and screening
  • Attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies
  • Low-maintenance for hot climates

Economic & Traditional Uses

  • Flowers used to make herbal tea with cooling properties
  • Plant parts used in tanning and natural dyeing
  • Traditional medicinal uses in local communities for skin care and health

Interesting Facts

  • Blooms almost year-round in tropical climates
  • The term “auriculata” refers to ear-shaped leaf base appendages
  • Often planted as a living fence
  • Extremely drought-tolerant once established

Cultivation & Care

  • Full sun exposure for best flowering
  • Water moderately; drought-tolerant once mature
  • Prefers well-drained soil
  • Prune after flowering to maintain shape
